an AI-written Reed–Solomon formalization holds up

the slackMCA_v4 Lean package in a third-party research repository, drafted by one AI system and packaged by another, formalizes the finitary core of a list-decoding paper: coefficient pigeonhole, quotient-core obstruction, cyclotomic and Fermat-field rigidity, one bad parameter per support. we rebuilt it at its pinned commit and asked the kernel: all 14 principal theorems rest only on the prover’s three standard axioms, nothing self-made. a statement-level review against the paper found every divergence a generalization, none a weakening. standing: release_verified; no human expert has reviewed it, and the report says so.

an audit that says no

a fully AI-authored repository claims a rank-four counterexample to Grothendieck's question: a finite locally free group scheme of order four that is not killed by four. at its first pinned commit, every extracted theorem is axiom-clean and the verdict is null: the kernel report shows the noncocommutativity theorem and the categorical power-map theorem absent from the source, and attestations for kernel obligations are refused. at a later commit both exist, axiom-clean, and the same machinery grants standing.

five finite theorems, kernel-checked, integrated upstream

five certificates produced by our proof pipeline live in the same third-party repository, integrated by its maintainer. one of them establishes that every element of the field with seventeen elements is a sum of two squares, checked exhaustively by the kernel; the others pin subgroup scales in the fields with seventeen and 257 elements, the size of an elementary-symmetric image, and a dithering window. each was ground-truthed by enumeration before formalization, and each carries its provenance.

a paper that audits itself

a dynamical-systems preprint whose central result is an exit guarantee: below explicit thresholds, every trajectory in the congested regime leaves through the cooperative boundary, never approaching the fold, in a time given exactly to leading order. eight results proved, four open problems stated as open. three legs of the remaining blow-up analysis are proven, the gluing between them remains open, and the paper's machine-readable claim table records exactly that. any byte change to the pdf flags every claim built on it until re-verified.

the machinery

five small open-source pieces produce the findings above: lean-introspect (proof-term and axiom extraction) · verification-events (hashed, replayable provenance) · csr-seed (claim identity and drift detection) · fold-registry (the fold paper's claim table) · standing-reports (the verdicts and their evidence).

about

manifold control is the practice of james kovalenko (charlottesville, va): independent verification of machine-generated work, with mathematics as the proving ground because there the checker is a proof kernel and every claim pins to bytes. when someone asks whether a result still holds, the answer is a build away.
